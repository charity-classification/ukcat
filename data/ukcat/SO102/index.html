Conflict resolution SO102¶

Conflict resolution is a tag in the UK-CAT classification of charities in the UK. It is part of the Society category.

338 charities have been tagged with this tag. This represents 0.17% of all charities.

Keywords to find charities¶

UK-CAT categories are applied to charities based on a set of keywords, in the form of a regular expression.

The regular expression used to find charities in this tag is:

\b(conflicts? (resolution|reconciliation))\b

The Ics&Rs Charitable Trust [Charity number: NI107131]
The Conflict Resolution Programme aims to develop an inclusive knowledge base that would help effectively address the major conflicts that affect countries or have the potential to do so. The conflicts could be at any level from local to national, regional and global. They could also be of varying degrees of intensity, ranging and complexity, from a relatively simple case of disagreements between two groups, to conflicts that involve multiple groups and dimensions. The development of an inclusive knowledge base calls for a fully integrated program to be taught which will cover all those who are affected directly or indirectly by a specific conflict. The programs taught will deliver conceptual innovations that will build on the latest developments in the conceptualisation of conflicts from across the world. The programs taught will achieve its objectives by being sensitive to the interface between the understanding of individual conflicts and the larger conceptualisation of conflict. It will aim to build an inclusive knowledge base and then disseminate that knowledge in a way that influences both popular perceptions and policies aimed at conflict resolution. The knowledge delivery will be carried out through short-term courses on conflict resolution, targeted at all who may have to deal with social conflicts, whether in industry, government or civil society. ICS&RS will achieve its purposes by the delivery of well-planned and integrated short courses, targeted to impart education and create awareness and public benefit. In furthering the purposes, it will co-operate with other charities & voluntary bodies to establish support. It will also employ staff to manage the knowledge delivery system and administration and grant scholarships, travelling and other bursaries to those who are likely to benefit. It will make grants and provide facilities for those wanting to study, which in long term will create jobs and relieve poverty.
